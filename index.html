Food Inflation & Catering Costs

An analysis for UK School Caterers | Autumn 2025

Overall Food & Non-Alcoholic Drink prices have risen by:

5.1%

in the 12 months to August 2025, the highest rate since early 2024.

Commodity Price Hotspots

Comparing the 12-month inflation rates for key catering commodities reveals significant cost pressures across the board, with proteins seeing the most dramatic increases. This data is essential for accurate menu costing and budget forecasting.

Spotlight on Major Increases

Beef & Veal

24.9%

Tight supply and continued high demand are the primary drivers for the dramatic rise in beef prices, impacting core menu items like mince and roasting joints.

Oils & Spreads

18.9%

Following dairy trends, butter prices have notably increased. Global supply chain issues continue to affect the cost of cooking oils and spreads.

Chicken

12.1%

While lower than red meat, poultry inflation is still significant due to rising feed, energy, and labour costs impacting the entire production chain.
